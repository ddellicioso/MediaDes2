Portfolio Prototype

Description

I made both low fidelity and high fidelity prototype for my portfolio so that I can test my ideas, gather some feedbacks and iterate my designs before start to implement them

What I Did

After making a quick sketch about the layout and everything, I directly went to make a low fidelity prototype in Figma to make a quick iterations and to see if I need to change or add anything. when I'm sure that it's good enough, I made the high fidelity.

When I was done I went to five teachers to ask about their opinions about it and to do user testing on them. So far when I showed them, they didn't have any problem with it (with any display, colors, or functionality) and majority of them had a very positive feedback about my idea.


Feedback

I showed Anke my layout and this are her feedbacks:

  • Pop up layout for the content might be too long and boring.
  • Consider an alternative to the pop up layout

Iteration

After the feedback I try to look for some inspirations in Pinterest and I start to sketch my idea. I showed my idea directly to Anke and she said that it's really nice and she likes the idea of the slideshow.
*more details and iterations in Learning Outcome 2 *

Reflection

How did it went: Creating both low fidelity and high fidelity prototypes for my portfolio was an enlightening experience that significantly contributed to the development process. Transitioning to a low fidelity prototype in Figma allowed me to make quick iterations and adjustments . I created a high fidelity version to help me present a more polished and realistic representation of the final product. To gather feedback and conduct user testing, I approached five different teachers. Their overwhelmingly positive feedback reassured me that the design was on the right track .

What did I learn: First I learned the value of iterative design ; moving from low fidelity to high fidelity prototypes underscored the importance of refining and validating ideas at each stage, sving time and effort in the long run. The importance of seeking feedback became clear as the teachers provided insights that I might overlooked.

What will change: Instead of just discussing new ideas, like the slideshow concept suggested by Anke, I would create additional low fidelity prototypes for all proposed changes.
