Project 1 : Client Interview

Description

We came to Geldrop once again to interview our clients to know and understand better what they actually wants because from the first presentation wasn’t really much of a help.

What I Did

The purpose of us conducting the interview to our client (Marianne and Cees) is to understand more about the current state and history of the orchestra. I decided to focus on several areas such as membership trends, reasons for decline, recruitment strategies, and tools used for promotion and communication. My goal is to identify current membership and historical trends, to understand the reasons behind changes in membership, explore recruitment efforts and effectiveness, and to learn about the best tools and strategies used for communication and engagement.

These are how I identify and learn about my goals:

  1. Identify current membership and historical trends
    • Conducting surveys with long-term members
    • Comparing current membership numbers with those from previous years
  2. Understanding reasons behind changes in membership
    • Examining external factors such as changes in the local community, economic conditions, or competition from other cultural events
  3. Exploring recruitment efforts and effectiveness
    • Reviewing past recruitment campaigns and initiatives
    • Analyzing data on the sources of new members (e.g. word-of-mouth, online ads, community events)
  4. Learning about strategies used for communication and engagement
    • Reviewing the organization’s website, social media channels, and email newsletters to see how they engage with members and the community

Thus, a survey directed to the existing members are conducted to answer these questions and analyze more.
*You can read more about the survey here!*

Reflection

How it went: Conducting the interview provided valuable insights into the orchestra's situation. It was clear that while they have a stable base of elderly members, attracting younger members remains a significant challenge. The interview revealed specific areas where they need support, such as improving their website and enhancing their social media presence.

What I learned: I learned the importance of making recruitment strategies for different age groups. For example, while workshops in schools may not yield immediate results, targeting elderly individuals through community events has been more successful. I also realized the necessity of being familiar with and effectively using digital tools like Wix and social media platforms to reach broader audiences.

What will change: Before the interview, I would research best practices for recruiting young members to orchestras and successful case studies from other organizations. I would also implement more structured interview techniques, such as a clear list of questions and follow-up prompts.
