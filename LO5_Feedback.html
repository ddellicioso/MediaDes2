Asking Feedbacks

Description

Asking for feedback is really helpful for both personal and professional growth. It shows I’m doing well and where I can get better, giving me useful tips to improve. Feedback make me able to see things from different perspectives and also help me spot any blind spots, especially about my designs. Positive feedback can boost my confidence and motivation, while constructive feedback helps me develop and make better decisions. It helps me keep improving my work quality can prevent conflicts by promoting transparency.

Reflection

What I learned: Through asking for feedback, I’ve realized how crucial it is for my personal and professional development. I learned that feedback provides a clear picture of my strengths and areas for improvement, offering valuable insights that I might miss otherwise. Positive feedback boosts my confidence and motivation, making me more eager to tackle challenges. On the other hand, constructive feedback helps me identify specific areas to develop, leading to better decision-making and continuous improvement in the quality of my work. Additionally, I’ve understood that seeking feedback can prevent potential conflicts by fostering transparency and open communication.

All of the feedbacks I received always provided benefits for me, for example for my portfolio and designs I made. By asking feedbacks, I was able to iterate my designs until it’s perfect. More detailed examples are in Learning Outcome 3!

Future Actions: Moving forward, I will make it a habit to regularly seek feedback from colleagues, mentors, and peers. I will actively listen to both positive and constructive comments, using them to guide my growth and development. I plan to implement the actionable insights I receive to enhance my skills and performance continually.
