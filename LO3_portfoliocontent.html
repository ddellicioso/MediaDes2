Portfolio Content Iterations

Description

I made some iterations regarding the content of my portfolio, since the first content wasn't clear enough for the teachers to read and grade me.

What I Did

After I found inspiration on Pinterest about what the layout of the content would look like, I made the sketch and implemented it in my portfolio.

Feedback 1

Chris came up to me and we discussed my portfolio and what to do to make it better:

  • Modify or replace the image carousel, as teachers found it difficult to review the content
  • Provide more structure and context to the content for better readability

Iteration 1

I was very fond of the image carousel, so I decided to keep it but modify how the learning outcomes were presented. I hoped this would resolve the issue of teachers having difficulty seeing the contents clearly.


Feedback 2

I texted Chris and showed him the new image carousel idea and asked if it was suitable. He provided the following feedback:

  • It hides the information behind slides, making teachers go back and forth.
  • There is a risk of missing information because it's hard to scan the content.

Iteration 2

Once I determined the best layout, I sketched it out to decide what elements to include. Fortunately, Chris provided some helpful tips on how to structure the content effectively. I then implemented these improvements directly into my portfolio.

Validating Iteration 2

Reflection

How it went: Initially, my content was not clear enough for the teachers to read and grade efficiently. By drawing inspirations from Pinterest and sketching a new layout, I made significant improvements. Chris's feedback was valuable, guiding me through several iterations.

What I learned: I learned the importance of clear and structured content in design. While creative elements like the carousel can be visually appealing, they should not compromise the usability and readability of the content. I also learned to balance my creative ideas with practical considerations to ensure that the final product meets the needs of its users.

What will change: I will prioritize clarity and usability from the outset. I will seek feedback early and often, so that my designs are both creative and functional. Additionally, I will be more flexible in letting go of design elements that do not serve the overall usability and readability of the content.
