Sprint X

Description

For my personal project, I decided to make a 3D scary character as a practice for my Blender skill *more details are here *. Before starting making the 3D character, I made a survey directed to Indonesia people to receive more authentic feedbacks *more details in LO4 *. Below are my journey making my first 3D character project.

What I Did

After I made the survey *here*, I looked into the responses I received and started roughly sketching the character based on the answers. background based on the result of the research and the survey. After the sketch of the character (body) was done, I moved to Illustrator to make the illustration clearer. It didn’t take long because I only made the lines cleaner and more visible so I can make the character in 3D easier.



Feedback 1

I asked for feedback regarding the final sketch (both the character’s body and the background) to Chris:

  • The character's shape is straightforward so it's easy to make
  • The background makes everything complete
  • Start blocking the scene with simple shapes and PNG images to see if the composition works

Iteration 1

After the feedback, first of all, I made the character in Blender with some help from YouTube. It was pretty easy to make since all I need to do is extrude and scale several times. Then I sculpted the face; I made hollow eye sockets, a nose, and sunken cheeks. I love sculpting so I really enjoyed making the face.

After making the face and the body, I searched for some PNG trees and put them in Blender to see the composition like Chris said.



Feedback 2

After the layout and the character are combined, I seek another feedback. So, I came to Jan (my semester coach) to show him the progress. I showed him my sketches, the survey, and the layout in Blender.

  • Suggested using sculpt to make the character’s cloth more realistic

Iteration 2

I did what I was told to do, make the cloth more realistic. Other than that, I started making the trees (regular trees and banana trees) with, of course, some tutorials. The hardest one to make was the banana trees; I needed to make a bunch of bananas, then the leaves, and finally combine all of them. The regular trees aren’t that hard to make.

Then I started putting textures (on the alley walls) and colors. On the alley walls, I put some moss to make it more realistic. It took quite a long time to make the moss and I was frustrated because the first time it didn’t work really well so I ended up taking a whole day to make that. But in the end, I managed to make it work.

Then I started putting lights to set the mood, such as: street lamps and atmospheric rays. After I put all those in, I’m quite happy with the result and it looks exactly like I want it to be.




Reflection

How it went: The process of creating a 3D scary character in Blender was both challenging and rewarding. From conducting surveys and research to sketching and sculpting, each step provided valuable insights and practical experience.
The process of creating a 3D scary character in Blender was both challenging and rewarding. From conducting surveys and research to sketching and sculpting, each step provided valuable insights and practical experience.

What I learned: Through this project, I learned several important lessons. Firstly, I realized the importance of planning and research before diving into the actual modeling. The survey results and my research on the characteristics of scary ghosts were crucial in shaping the final character design. I also discovered that sketching and refining those sketches in Illustrator made the 3D modeling process much smoother.

One of the most significant technical skills I honed was sculpting in Blender. I particularly enjoyed sculpting the face, learning how to create hollow eye sockets, a nose, and sunken cheeks to give the character a spooky look. The feedback to make the character’s clothing more realistic taught me the value of fine details in enhancing the overall appearance of the model.

The process of adding textures and lighting was a big learning curve. Creating realistic textures, especially the moss on the alley walls, was challenging but ultimately rewarding. This experience underscored the importance of perseverance and attention to detail.

What will change: Moving forward, I aim to improve my texturing and lighting techniques further. While I was happy with the final result, I know there’s always room for improvement. I will explore more advanced tutorials and practice different methods to enhance the realism of my models.

Finally, I will embrace challenges with a positive mindset, knowing that persistence and problem-solving are key to success in 3D modeling. This project has boosted my confidence and reinforced my passion for 3D design, motivating me to continue pursuing my dream job in this field.
