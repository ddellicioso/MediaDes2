Project 1 : Orchestra Member Survey

Description

This survey is conducted to dig deeper into what makes people join orchestra and what keeps them there. I covered stuff like how they found out about orchestras, when they started playing, why they love it, and where they get their info. Plus, I wanted to know how social media and parents play into it all.

What I Did

I put together this survey with questions covering everything from how people first heard about orchestras to why they decided to join. To keep things simple, I offered multiple-choice options and showed percentages to make it easy to understand . The survey covered a wide range of topics, including when people started playing, what they love most about being in an orchestra, and where they get their info about music events. I aimed to make the survey fun and easy to fill out to encourage more participation.

Overall, I got some really cool insights from the survey. It was interesting to see how many people find out about orchestras through social media these days, and how much their parents influence their musical journey. Now that I have all this data, I can start figuring out ways to make orchestra life even better for everyone involved. Maybe I'll focus more on social media outreach or find ways to make orchestras more family-friendly.

Since the majority of people found out about the orchestra through social media, I relayed the data to my teammates; and from these data I acquired, my team was able to make couple of products, such as social media planning, posters, and a better website *You can see the prototype in LO 1 here *.

Reflection

How it went: The survey went really well overall. I received good response rate and gathered a lot of valuable insights into orchestra membership. The survey gave a deep understanding of how people find out about orchestras, when they start getting into music, what they love most about being part of an orchestra, and what keeps them coming back for more.

What I learned: I learned about how significant social media has become in people’s discovery of orchestras. It’s clear that platforms like Facebook and Instagram play a big role in reaching potential members. Additionally, it was eye-opening to see the strong influence parents have on their children's musical journeys.

What will change: I'll likely put more focus on social media outreach, maybe even investing in targeted ads or partnering with influencers. Additionally, I want to explore ways to better involve parents in the orchestra community, perhaps by offering special events or resources tailored to them.
