Project 3 : User Test

Description

This user test is conducted to test our final prototype of our project before stepping into implementation and as well as observing any potential issues. The test serves as a small framework into figuring out what needed to stay/change.

What I Did

I used CMD Methods to conduct the user test, which is Usability Testing. Before I conduct the test, I made sure to define my objectives to set clear goals on what I wanted to learn from the usability testing, such as:

  • Is the new content appealing to the intended audience?
  • Does the content structure and layout align with the target audience's requirements?
  • Can users easily find the easter egg?

Since my group’s target audience is Dutch teenagers and their parents, we weren’t able to find such participants so we decided to recruit some Dutch students around Fontys. When I conducted the tests, I had users manually interact with the product while I’m observing their actions and noted any issues.

After users are done, I collected detailed feedback about their experience, any difficulties they encountered, and their overall satisfaction. I then reviewed the feedback and observations to identify patterns, common issues, and areas for improvement; then compiled the results into a report, highlighting key findings for enhancing the usability of the website.

Feedback

After conducting user tests, we received numerous feedbacks such as:

  • "Apply Now" button didn't work
  • Incosistency on the icon in the header
  • Users able to find and interact with the easter egg
  • Adding underlines on "Contact Us" links when hovered to enhance visibility

*Iteration of the design are in Learning Outcome 3*

Reflection

How it went: The user test provided valuable insights into the effectiveness of our final prototype and highlighted areas for improvement before implementation. Overall, the test went smoothly, allowing us to observe user interactions and gather detailed feedback on functionality, user experience, and design effectiveness.

Through the use of CMD Methods, specifically Usability Testing, we were able to gather comprehensive feedback from users. This included insights into whether the new content resonated with the intended audience, whether the structure and layout met their needs, and whether users could easily find the easter egg.

What I learned: From the feedback received, I learned of several issues that need attention. The non-functioning "Apply Now" button and inconsistencies in the header icons are critical issues that require immediate resolution. On a positive note, users were able to find and interact with the hidden surprise, indicating its successful implementation.

What will change: Moving forward, I will prioritize addressing the identified issues to ensure a seamless user experience. This may involve troubleshooting technical issues, refining design elements for consistency, and implementing suggested enhancements such as underlining "Contact us" links for improved visibility.

Overall, the user test was instrumental in identifying areas for refinement and validating the effectiveness of our prototype. By addressing the feedback received, I aim to enhance the usability and functionality of our project, ultimately delivering a more satisfying experience for our users.
