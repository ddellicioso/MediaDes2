Learning Blender

Description

My efforts to learn Blender by watching tutorials in YouTube

What I Did

Cake

This is the first time I ever touched Blender and I've always wanted to try to make some 3D things. I wanted to be a horror game designer so that means I want to be in Game Design specialization later in semester 4.

The first time I used Blender, so of course I looked into some YouTube tutorials and I found a popular tutorial from Blender Guru. The tutorial showed how to make a donut but I didn't want to just blindly follow him so I decided to make a tart cake instead.

So far, I really liked sculpting and I found myself enjoying it so much. Unfortunately, lately I didn't have any time to continue my cake even though I really want to start learning about coloring, texturing, and more.

Update on my cake:

I learned some more things after watching more tutorials about texture paint, geometry nodes, and weight paint. Texture paint was easy to do and quite fun but I still don't understand the placement of the color on the "paper" so I need to dive deeper into this part.

Geometry nodes are hard . I used geometry nodes to make the sprinkles (density and colors). I don't understand how those become like that and how do I know which one I should tweak? Sadly, the YouTube tutorial I watched wasn't much of help and I ended up blindly following it without actually understanding what those meant.

To make the sprinkles' density more realistically placed, I learned and used weight paint. The red areas are for more concentrated sprinkles while the blue ones mean fewer sprinkles . It wasn't hard to follow up the tutorial and understand it.

After all of the tweaks, I finally finished the cake. It doesn't look that appetizing but I'm proud of the finished look; at least I tried my best to make it my own cake. I rendered it with GPU and it lags so much, and I don't know if that's a normal thing or not.

Throughout the entire process, I'm glad my laptop never crashed , not even once, and I didn't have to do all of the process all over again. Even though my laptop hasn't crashed, I hope I could've saved the file more often to prevent such a thing from happening.

Overall, I'm pretty satisfied with the result and the amount of new knowledge I got by making this cake and watching tutorials. Though, I still need to dive deeper and learn more about texture paint and especially geometry nodes .

Kitchen Diorama

Other than watching YouTube, I also joined Blender workshops at school. Currently we are learning on how to make a kitchen diorama, and it was fun and not too complex and intimidating for me so I really enjoyed it.

I used "Shear" tool to make the fridge's dispenser look more real; the shear tool makes the dispenser tilt . Then I constructed a window near the fridge to gave it more accent in the diorama. I didn't know how to efficiently make a window, so I just did what I think is logical; in the end it works, but if we look at it from "edit mode" it looks messy .

Then I put some chairs and a table , and gave it some colors on the fridge, stove, etc. To be honest, the rendered result looked like it was made by an AI (or that's what I feel like). Other than that, I'm quite happy with the result even though I didn't really learn anything new but I'm glad I can use my knowledge and do more practice.




Reflection

How it went: My first individual practice into Blender was both exciting and challenging. Inspired by the Blender Guru donut tutorial, I decided to create a tart cake as my first project. Sculpting turned out to be enjoyable, and I was eager to learn more about coloring and texturing, but time constraints made it difficult to progress as quickly as I wanted. Despite these challenges, I managed to learn about texture paint, geometry nodes, and weight paint through additional YouTube tutorials. Texture painting was relatively straightforward, but geometry nodes proved to be quite complex. Nevertheless, I applied weight paint successfully to create realistic sprinkles on my cake.

In addition to my YouTube-based learning, I participated in Blender workshops at school, where I worked on a kitchen diorama. This project was less complex and allowed me to practice my skills with tools like the shear tool for more realistic modeling. While I didn't learn many new concepts during this project, it was a good opportunity to apply my existing knowledge. My teachers provided valuable feedback on my cake, suggesting improvements and recommending better tutorial channels, which was both encouraging and motivating.

What I learned: Throughout this journey, I discovered a passion for sculpting , finding it both therapeutic and enjoyable. I learned the basics of texture painting but realized the need for a deeper understanding of color placement. Geometry nodes were particularly challenging, indicating a need for further study .

What will change: I will dedicate more time to understanding geometry nodes through better tutorial channels, I will implement a habit of saving my work more frequently to prevent data loss. Lastly, I will continue working on practical projects to apply my skills in different contexts and improve my modeling techniques.
