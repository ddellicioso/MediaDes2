Project Media Development

Description

We already made a figma prototype from the previous project (I wasn't in the prototype team) so we just extracted what we liked and agreed on. After that we made a new Figma prototype to have a general idea and themes/colors to match Fontys' brandguide. The next step is to make a user *put a link* research survey or A/B Testing focused on our target audience (Dutch teenagers) to see which designs or contents they like/want to see more.

What I Did

My part is to make the profile page (Media Design). In the survey, I asked the respondents to pick which design and colors they like the most as well as asking them about how they think the content should be arranged like.

I made several iterations on the page (ver1 is what we (our team) like, ver2 what teachers think should be, ver3/final is the modified version according to the answers of our target audience) *more detailed explanation in LO**.

Feedback 1

We approached Jan for feedback on our prototype. Here's what he suggested:

  • Shorten the pages to reduce user scrolling time
  • Contact Us section'c color is not suitable; feels out of place
  • Likes the pink header

Iteration 1

After the feedback, I changed the color of the header and the contact us section.
For the header I decided to use pink because it matches with Fontys' brandguide , as well as changed the font's weight.
For the contact us section, I changed the color into purple so it mached with the rest of the page's color. I also replaced the box in the contact us section into a form so people can write their messages directly without having to go to their email.

Validating iteration 1

Feedback 2 — Survey answers

After we got answers from our survey, I compiled answers for the profile page, the answers are:

  • Semester and Example Project needs to be separated
  • Button are better if it's purple
  • Purple Contact Us has more vote
  • Job Opportunity are nicer in "card" layout

Iteration 2 (from survey answers)

*More detailed explanations and iteratons in Learning Outcome 3*
I fixed the designs and contents exactly like the results of the survey. I separated the Semester planning and Example Project, I made the button into purple, and changed the layout of the Job Opportunity section into cards in a caraousel.

Validating iteration 2

Reflection

How did it go : Surprisingly, it went really good despite the amount of time we had to do user tests, user research, and iterations of the prototype. We also received pretty much good feedbacks from people we tested on. Though sometimes we lacked of initiative as a group but still we managed to finish pretty nicely.

What did I learn: I leant that user test/research is really helpful to decide which design I need to implement because I had a tough time to decide which one to use and make.

What will change: I'm going to do user test/research faster and early at the begining of making the prototype so I can reduce the time wasted.
