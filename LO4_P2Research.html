Project 2: Research Question

Description

This research aims to assess whether the material provided by Fontys resonates with Dutch students and their parents, the target audience. By applying CMD methods, the study investigates how effectively Fontys' materials meet the informational needs and preferences of this audience.

What I Did

  1. Library Literature Study:
    • Examined existing materials and resources provided by Fontys, including Open Days, webinars, and social media presence on Instagram and Facebook.
    • Evaluated the Fontys website, identifying issues such as inconsistent margins and insufficient information on available courses and activities
  2. Field Research:
    • Conducted surveys with ICT students at Fontys, gathering 47 responses from five different profiles.
      • Findings: Instagram and TikTok were the most preferred social media platforms; the Fontys website was deemed most helpful.
    • Conducted a survey targeting teenagers and parents. (*not my part)
      • Teenagers: Found the school website helpful; desired information on job opportunities, semester planning, and example projects; cited unorganized information as an obstacle.
      • Parents: Used the school website for gathering information; wanted details on courses and application processes; found unorganized and overwhelming layouts disturbing.

Reflection

How it went: Conducting surveys with both students and parents helped gather diverse perspectives , while the literature study offered a foundational understanding of Fontys' current communication strategies.

What I learned: Students are keen on knowing job opportunities, semester planning, and example projects. Parents prioritize course details and application processes. Both students and parents find unorganized information and overwhelming layouts on the website to be significant obstacles.

What will change: I would expand the survey distribution to include a larger and more diverse sample of students and parents to gain more comprehensive insights. I'd conduct usability testing of the Fontys website with both students and parents to identify specific pain points and areas for improvement.
