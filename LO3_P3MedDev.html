Fontys Website Iteration

Description

We had a previous Figma prototype from an earlier project (I wasn't part of the prototype team) and extracted elements we liked and agreed upon. Then, we created a new Figma prototype to establish a general idea and theme/colors aligned with Fontys' brand guide. The next step was conducting a user research survey or A/B Testing targeting Dutch teenagers to determine their preferred designs and content.

What I Did

My role was to design the profile page (Media Design). In the survey, I asked respondents to select their preferred design and colors and provided insights on how the content should be organized.

I created multiple iterations of the page (version 1 based on our team's preferences, version 2 reflecting teachers' suggestions, and the final version incorporating feedback from our target audience)
*also mentioned in Learning Outcome 1 *.

Feedback 1

We approached Jan for feedback on our prototype. Here are his suggestions:

  • Agreed with separating the semester planning and example projects for clarity
  • Appreciated the purple button for its eye-catching appeal
  • Favored the "card" style for Job Opportunities because it is visually appealing and avoids long texts

Iteration 1

Based on the feedback, I made several changes:
I changed the header color to pink to align with Fontys' brand guide and adjusted the font weight.
For the Contact Us section, I changed the color to purple to match the rest of the page. I also replaced the box with a form so users can directly write their messages without needing to go to their email.

Validating Iteration 1

I asked a friend to validate the changes, and this was her feedback:

  • The pink header is more prominent and eye-catching
  • Good content structure
  • The Contact Us section looks better in purple, matching the rest of the page
  • Removing the unnecessary photo from the old Contact Us section was a good move
  • The contact form is an improvement because fewer steps to contact make it more user-friendly

Feedback (2) from survey

I compiled the answers from the survey we conducted, where people were asked about their preferred designs and content structure.

  • Wants a straightforward, easy to understand layout
  • Balance texts and images
  • Use more color to make the layout more visually appealing
  • The structure of the content would be: description of the profile, semester planning, example project, example schedule, job opportunities, and contact us; respectively.

Iteration 2

After receiving the answers, I directly fixed the prototype to match the survey results. I separated the semester planning from the example project, changed the button to purple, as well as the contact us section, and also revised the career prospects section.

Validating Iteration 2

Another friend reviewed and validated the second version:

  • Agreed that separating the semester planning and example projects makes the content more straightforward
  • Appreciated the purple button for being more eye-catching
  • Liked the "card" style for Job Opportunities, noting that most people are attracted to visuals rather than long texts

Reflection

How it went : Remarkably, the process unfolded exceptionally well, despite the significant time invested in user tests, research, and prototype iterations. Feedback from our test participants was predominantly positive. Although there were instances where our group lacked initiative, we still managed to complete the project satisfactorily.

What I learned: I discovered the immense value of user testing and research in guiding design decisions, particularly during challenging moments when choosing and refining designs.

What will change: Moving forward, I intend to initiate user testing and research earlier in the prototype development process, aiming to minimize time wastage.
