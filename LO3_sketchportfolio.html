Portfolio Sketch & Layout

Description

I developed a patisserie concept with the primary color being my favorite, pink. Initially, I sketched my ideas on Procreate and sought feedback right away. I used Illustrator to create the elements and graphics, and planned to use Adobe XD for upcoming prototypes.

What I Did

I started by sketching my ideas in Procreate, then created the design elements and graphics in Illustrator. To refine my concept, I used feedback from teachers and iterated on my design. For the prototype, I planned to use Adobe XD to bring everything together. Based on feedback, I devised the recipe book idea, which redirects users to another page when they click on the learning outcome button, ensuring a clear layout for easy reading.

I was searching for some inspiration in Pinterest and I found a cute drawings of a store. So I started by sketching my ideas in Procreate. I actually wanted the website to be 3D but I know that it will too hard for me in this semester besides I will have several projects to do.

Feedback 1

I came up to Maikel to show him my ideas for my portfolio

  • Suggested to use "layering" technique to mimick 3D style
  • Suggested to start making a color scheme
  • Prepare a fallback if things go wrong
  • Make a storyboard for clearer ideas

Iteration 1

I made a storyboard (minus annotation) about the portfolio, I put things that I wanted to be shown and I also made a simple color pallete. After I know what colors I should use and the details of the rooms, I made a low fidelity and an interactive high fidelity prototypes.

Validating Iteration 1 (and some feedbacks)

After I made the prototypes I went to 5 teachers to test out the user interactivity in my Figma prototype and these are what they said:

  • Unique and good idea
  • Make the arrow more visible
  • Try to not use pop-up for the learning outcome
  • Try to not dwell too much on the designing part
  • Doable and feels personal

So overall none of the teachers had major problem navigating through my prototypes, but still I need to fix some details to enhance the experience more.

Iteration 2

To fix the pop-up problem, I go to Pinterest to look for references on what layout I should try to make. Then I found like a recipe paper that is quite cute so I tried to sketch what would it look like in the portfolio.

Validating Iteration 2

After I finished sketching I directly went to Anke (as she was the one gave the feedback about the pop-up thing). I showed her the sketch and she likes the idea of the image carousel and also the feedback and reflection sections.

Feedback 3

I had a talk with Chris regarding the content *see details in here * and the layout of my portfolio.

  • Fix or change the image carousel because the teaches had a hard time checking the content
  • Content are not clear
  • Should give more structure in the content

Iteration 3

I really liked the image carousel so I decided to try to keep it but change how I put the content of the learning outcomes and hopefully it fixed the problem (teachers couldn't see the contents clearly).

Feedback 4

I texted Chris and showed him the new image carousel idea and asked him if it's alright if I use that,

  • It hides the informations behind slides and it makes the teacher to go back and forth
  • Risk of them missing informations because it's hard to scan the content

So then I had an idea to structure the content in a PDF way, and again I asked him about what he thinks about it. He agreed with the PDF layout and I should focus on the structure.

Iteration 4

After knowing what layout would work and better than the rest, I sketched it out to see what are the things I should put and fortunately Chris gave me some hints on how I should structure so it can come out nicely. Then I directly implemented it in my portfolio

Validating Iteration 4

Reflection

How it went: The process of developing my patisserie concept and iterating on the design was both challenging (and tiring) and rewarding. Initially, sketching my ideas in Procreate and refining them in Illustrator helped me visualize the final product. The feedbacks I received from teachers was valuable in guiding the design towards a more polished and user friendly interface.

What I learned: I learned the importance of starting with a solid foundation, such as creating color scheme, storyboard, and initial sketches. This provided a clear direction and structure for the project. Each round of feedback provided insights that helped improve the design, particularly in making the layout more intuitive and user friendly

What will change: If I were need to do this again, I would do feedbacks more systematically, making sure that I validate each iteration with user testing to identify and address potential issues early on. Furthermore, I will balance my enthusiasm for creative elements, like the image carousel, with practical considerations to ensure that content is always accessible and easy to navigate. By focusing on clear structure and usability, I want to create designs that are both visually appealing and highly functional.
