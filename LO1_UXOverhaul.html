UX Overhaul

Description

On the third day of the intro week, we were introduced to UX design, it basically is making UX easy to use for all kinds of people. A lot of things were showcased during the session, and we got to learn UX design’s core principles, the role of user research and testing, as well as what visual elements help improve the user experience (and what to avoid).

By the end of it, we were given an assignment, highlighting the key factors in what makes a good and bad UX design. We were first told to look for a bad UX design, and with all the things we learned earlier, we were tasked to improve the design as much as possible. We were also told to poison an example of a good UX design, where we get to basically do the opposite, and try to make it as bad as possible.

What I Did

I looked into Can’t Unsee and User Inyerface to experience both bad and good UX design; as a result, I made compiled key points about what makes a bad/good UX design after we looked into websites in Miro board.

After writing down my findings, I looked into one of my favorite bookstores in Indonesia called Periplus (as an example of bad UX design). I noticed that Periplus has few bad visual elements such as inconsistent styling of the dropdowns, bad alignment of the placeholder and sign-in text, and low resolution of the logo. So I moved to Figma to fix all of those things I mentioned, like using the same style for the dropdowns, fixing the alignment, and fixing the resolution of the logo.
*here is the Figma prototype for an example of bad to good design

For the example of good design, I browsed through Awwwards and found a website I liked called Hajster Climate System. The website has really nice and consistent fonts, a good color palette, a good call-to-action button, and a nice color contrast. To make the website turn into a bad design, I used random colors for the texts, messed with the fonts on the header, navigations, and the call-to-action button, and lastly, I made the website have bad color contrast.
*Figma prototype of good to bad design



Feedback

I came up to Maikel to get feedback on both my good and bad UX design. Overall, he said to use some user research to enhance the user usability as well as to validate that I really did fix the website. Here's what he said:

  • Make/use user research to enhance user usability
  • Good job on paying attention to details (for Periplus website)
  • Didn’t really stay in style with the original website (for the bad to good design) but still it’s a good work

Reflection

How it went: This assignment was a highly engaging and insightful experience. I started by diving into good and bad UX design using websites like Can’t Unsee and User Inyerface. These helped me get a clear idea of what works and what doesn’t in UX design. For my assignment, I picked the Periplus bookstore website as an example of bad UX. I found issues like inconsistent dropdown styles, misaligned text, and a low-res logo. Using Figma, I fixed these problems, making the design more consistent and visually appealing. On the flip side, I chose the Hajster Climate System website from Awwwards to show how good UX can turn bad. This site had great design elements, but I messed it up by using random colors, changing fonts inconsistently, and messing with color contrast.

What I learned: I learned that keeping styles consistent across a site is crucial. When things like fonts and colors are all over the place, it confuses users and hurts the site’s usability. Good designs use visual hierarchy to guide users’ attention to important things like buttons, messing this up can make the site hard to use.

What will change: Next time, I’ll include more user research in my process. This means:

  • Running surveys and usability tests to get direct feedback
  • Continuously tweaking designs based on what users say and do
