Future Goal and How to Achieve

I’ve always wanted to be a game designer, especially horror game. I love watching people playing horror games and I dreamed to make a psychological VR horror game (because I think that’s even scarier). I was really happy that I get to learn about Blender in this semester because it means I’m a step closer to being a game designer, which also leads to my Sprint X project decision.

For Sprint X *here’s the link for the planning*, I decided to make use of it as a practice for my Blender skill; I’ll be making a 3D horror character from one of the most famous ghosts from Indonesia, Pocong. At first, I thought of making Leak (a mythical creature from Bali, Indonesia) but after talking to Chris, it’ll be too hard for me to make it with my skill right now. My goal for this project is that my skill in Blender will increase and will broaden my knowledge about designing 3D characters.

In semester 4, I’m planning to go to Game Design specialization and for the Minor program, I’m planning to go either VR minor or something about psychology. For an internship, I joined Career Day in TQ and I found one company called LiveWall that is really interesting and kind of fits my goal. I talked to them about the possibility of internships, how they work in the company, what language they are using to make games (it’s unity), etc. Even though they are not specifically about ‘horror’ games, but I’m pretty sure if I get there, I will get more experiences about games.

Self Reflection

Most of my skills right now are more into web developer (HTML, CSS); while my goal is to be a game designer. Fortunately, this semester taught me about Blender which will help me achieve my dream. I am doing everything I can to learn more about Blender and designing, which is why my Sprint X project is to make a scary character.

One of the hardest personal challenges I’ve encountered was time management. At the start of the semester, I decided to join an organization called Eindhoven League *see in LO3 and LO5*, and that took a lot of my time that I could’ve used to do my school work. For quite some time, I couldn’t catch up with the amount of school work I needed to do and this moment really stressed me out.

After having a talk with my friend (she has nice time management), she gave me some tips such as, breaking tasks into smaller steps, making a to-do list, and eliminate distractions. After implementing the tips my friend gave me, I started getting the hang of my projects. I started to learn to be more productive and use my time wisely, which made me able to finish my tasks properly and on schedule.
