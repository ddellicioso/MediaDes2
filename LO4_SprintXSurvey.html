Sprint X : Survey

Description

I made the survey to receive some insights on how I should design my scary character. I explicitly spread the survey among Indonesian people to gain authentic answers and vision.

What I Did

My first step was browsing through the internet to look at some references on how Pocong (the scary character) is supposed to look. I watched some (supposedly) “found” footage of Pocong to get an idea of their behavior and where they are usually found. After that, I started making the questions to be put in the survey.

The questions included the design of its appearance (eyes, skin color, shroud condition, etc.), their body posture, and the background (their location). I shared the survey with all of my Indonesian friends.

The result of the survey was more or less the same as what I expected. Even though I got fewer responses than my goal, the answers were still very helpful for my design choices.

Reflection

How it went: The process of creating and distributing the survey to gain insights on designing the scary character, Pocong, went relatively well. Initially, I conducted thorough research by browsing various references and watching supposed “found” footage to understand Pocong's typical appearance and behavior. This research phase was crucial in forming the basis of the survey questions, which focused on various aspects of the character's design such as eyes, skin color, shroud condition, body posture, and typical locations.

After drafting the questions, I distributed the survey among my Indonesian friends to ensure the feedback was authentic and culturally relevant. While the number of responses was lower than anticipated, the quality of the responses was high and provided valuable insights that aligned closely with my initial expectations.

What I learned: The initial research phase was important in creating relevant and specific survey questions. Understanding the cultural context and typical features of Pocong helped in formulating questions that would yield useful insights. Ensuring that the survey was spread among Indonesian people was crucial for obtaining authentic and culturally relevant feedback. This reinforced the importance of understanding and respecting cultural contexts in design projects.

What will change: While maintaining a focus on cultural relevance, I will also consider expanding the demographic scope to include different age groups, regions, and backgrounds within Indonesia to gain a more comprehensive understanding of perceptions about Pocong.
