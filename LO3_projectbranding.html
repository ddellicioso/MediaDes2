Project Branding Brandguide

Description

We made a brandguide for the client and we tasked Sanne (one of our teammates) to make the first brandguide . We put colors of the brand, some typography, rules of the logo, etc.

Feedback 1 (on the 1st brandguide)

We had a second presentation regarding this project and we needed to show what we did for the last few weeks, so we printed and showed the clients our brandguide.

  • They don't like the header font we used because it looks old fashioned
  • The logo is already good
  • They liked the mock-ups
  • Color palette is nice

Iteration 1

My teammates assigned me to fix the brandguide. Sanne (one of my teammates) used Adobe InDesign to make the first brandguide and I've never touched nor used InDesign before so it felt quite intimidating but Sanne is kind enough to help me figure out what to do.

We used another font for the header that is more modern and flexible and still fits with the logo and colors. I had to re-make the mock-ups in Photoshop and fortunately it didn't take long to fix. I also added a tone of voice in the brandguide after we had a lecture about it. We made the tone of voice based on the feeling that we get from the clients' vibes and also from the fonts, and typography that we used.

Reflection

How it went: The initial feedback on our brandguide was mixed. While the logo and mock-ups were well-received, the header font was criticized for being old-fashioned. This feedback highlighted the need for a more modern and cohesive design. With the help of my teammate, Sanne, I tackled the task of updating the brandguide despite my initial unfamiliarity with Adobe InDesign. The new font and added tone of voice improved the overall presentation and alignment with the client's preferences.

What I learned: This experience taught me the importance of being adaptable and open to constructive criticism. Learning to use new tools like InDesign under tight deadlines was challenging but ultimately rewarding. Additionally, I realized the significance of aligning design elements, such as fonts and color palettes, with the brand's identity and client's vision. Incorporating a tone of voice also added depth to our brandguide, making it more professional and engaging.

What will change: In future projects, I will prioritize gathering and incorporating feedback early in the design process to avoid major revisions later on. I will also continue to expand my skill set with design tools to enhance my versatility and efficiency. Ensuring that all design elements are modern, cohesive, and reflective of the brand's identity will be a key focus. By doing so, I want to create more polished and client-aligned deliverables from the outset.
